## Common Repairs and Parts
So, what are some of the most common repairs and parts that you may need to address on your Surface Pro? Here are a few examples:

* **Screen repairs**: If you've dropped your Surface Pro or subjected it to other forms of physical stress, you may need to replace the screen. This can be a relatively straightforward process, but it requires careful attention to detail to avoid damaging other components.
* **Battery replacements**: Over time, your Surface Pro's battery may begin to degrade, reducing its overall lifespan and performance. Replacing the battery can be a bit more involved, but it's still a feasible DIY project for those with basic technical skills.
* **Port repairs**: The Surface Pro's ports can be prone to damage, especially if you're frequently plugging and unplugging devices. If you notice that a particular port is no longer functioning, you may need to replace it or clean out any debris that may be causing the issue.
* **Storage upgrades**: If you're running low on storage space or want to upgrade to a faster SSD, you may need to replace your Surface Pro's storage drive. This can be a relatively simple process, but it requires careful attention to compatibility and installation procedures.

When it comes to sourcing replacement parts, there are a few options available. You can purchase official Microsoft parts, which are often more expensive but guarantee compatibility and quality. Alternatively, you can opt for third-party parts, which may be more affordable but require more research and verification to ensure compatibility.
