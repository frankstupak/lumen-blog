## Introduction
As a Linux user who owns a Microsoft Surface Pro, you're likely no stranger to the world of refurbishing and reselling devices. Whether you're looking to upgrade to a newer model or simply want to make some extra cash, wiping and refurbishing your Surface Pro is a great way to breathe new life into an old device. ## Preparing for Refurbishment
Before you start the refurbishment process, it's essential to back up any important data you may have on your device. This includes documents, photos, videos, and any other files you don't want to lose. You can use a cloud storage service like Google Drive or Dropbox to upload your files, or use an external hard drive like the [Samsung T7 Shield Portable SSD](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B09QBN6HCJ?tag=tinywhale-20) to store your data locally.

Once you've backed up your data, it's time to wipe your device clean. This will remove all of your personal files, settings, and installed software, leaving you with a blank slate. To do this, you'll need to boot into the UEFI firmware settings and select the "Reset" option. This will erase all data on the device, so make sure you've backed up everything you need before proceeding.

## Reinstalling Linux
With your device wiped clean, it's time to reinstall Linux. You can use a distribution like Ubuntu or Debian, or opt for a more lightweight option like Lubuntu or Xubuntu. Regardless of which distribution you choose, you'll need to create a bootable USB drive using a tool like Rufus or Etcher.

Once you've created your bootable USB drive, insert it into your Surface Pro and reboot. You'll need to enter the UEFI firmware settings and select the USB drive as the boot device. From there, follow the on-screen instructions to install Linux on your device.

## Optimizing Your Device for Resale
With Linux installed, it's time to optimize your device for resale. This includes updating the kernel and installing any necessary drivers, as well as configuring the device for maximum performance.
