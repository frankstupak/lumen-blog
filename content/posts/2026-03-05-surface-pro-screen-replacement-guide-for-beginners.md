## Introduction
As a Linux user who owns a Microsoft Surface Pro, you're likely no stranger to the world of refurbishment and DIY repairs. ## Preparing for the Replacement
Before you start the replacement process, it's essential to prepare your device and gather the necessary tools. First, make sure you have a backup of your important files and data. ## Removing the Old Screen
Removing the old screen is the most challenging part of the replacement process. You'll need to use a combination of tools, including a suction cup, a spudger, and a screwdriver. First, apply the suction cup to the screen and gently pry it away from the bezel. Be careful not to apply too much pressure, as you don't want to damage the surrounding components.

Once you've removed the screen, you'll need to disconnect the display cable and the digitizer cable. Use a spudger to carefully pry the cables away from the screen, and then use a screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the cables in place.

## Installing the New Screen
Installing the new screen is a relatively straightforward process. First, connect the display cable and the digitizer cable to the new screen. Make sure the cables are securely connected and not damaged in any way.

Next, apply a small amount of adhesive to the bezel and attach the new screen. Make sure the screen is properly aligned and the adhesive is evenly distributed.

## Reassembling the Device
Once the new screen is installed, you can start reassembling the device. First, reconnect the display cable and the digitizer cable to the motherboard. Then, replace the screws that hold the cables in place.

Next, replace the bezel and the screen surround. Make sure the bezel is properly aligned and the screen surround is securely attached.
